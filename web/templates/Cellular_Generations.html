Cellular Generations

1G - Analog Cellular

The first generation of cellular systems introduced analog technology. It enabled basic voice communication and had limited capacity and coverage.

2G - Digital Cellular

The second generation marked the shift from analog to digital technology. It introduced digital voice transmission and enabled SMS (Short Message Service).

3G - Mobile Broadband

The third generation brought significant improvements, including higher data transfer rates and the ability to access the internet. It enabled services such as video calling and mobile internet browsing.

4G - LTE

The fourth generation, also known as LTE (Long-Term Evolution), offered faster data speeds, low latency, and improved multimedia support. It revolutionized mobile internet and paved the way for advanced services like HD video streaming and online gaming.

5G - Next-Generation Connectivity

The fifth generation is the latest advancement in cellular technology. 5G networks provide ultra-fast speeds, extremely low latency, and massive device connectivity. It promises transformative applications like autonomous vehicles, remote surgeries, and the Internet of Things (IoT).
